<p><p>Dental liners serve as a protective barrier between restorative materials and the pulp of the tooth, helping to prevent irritation and inflammation. Two common types of dental liners are Glass Ionomer and Calcium Hydroxide. Glass Ionomer liners release fluoride, aiding in the prevention of decay and promoting remineralization. Calcium Hydroxide liners have antibacterial properties and stimulate the formation of reparative dentin. Both types of liners are essential in maintaining the health and longevity of dental restorations.</p></p>
