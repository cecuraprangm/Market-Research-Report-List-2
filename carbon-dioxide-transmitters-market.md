<p><p>Carbon dioxide transmitters are used to monitor and measure levels of CO2 in various applications. There are two main types of transmitters in the market - Infrared and Heat Conduction. Infrared transmitters use infrared light to detect CO2 levels, while Heat Conduction transmitters rely on measuring the heat generated by the absorption of CO2. Each type has its own advantages and limitations, making them suitable for different environments and requirements. Both types play a crucial role in maintaining indoor air quality and ensuring safety in various industries.</p></p>
