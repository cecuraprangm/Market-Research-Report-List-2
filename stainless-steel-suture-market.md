<p><p>Stainless steel sutures are classified into two main types: monofilament and braided. Monofilament sutures are made of a single strand of stainless steel, providing high tensile strength and minimal tissue reaction. On the other hand, braided sutures consist of multiple strands intertwined together, offering increased flexibility and handling. Both types are widely used in various surgical procedures due to their durability, resistance to corrosion, and ability to maintain tissue approximation.</p></p>
